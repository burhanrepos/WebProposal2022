  4. Problem with Manual System and it’s issues

    5. o “Manual Systems are the Systems in which data is stored, managed and used manually.” For Example, Manual Joining System, Manual Seat Reservation System of Bus Company.

    o In today’s day and age, online shopping is becoming increasingly popular, especially during the COVID-19 crisis, where people became stranded inside of their homes, online shopping came out as an only solution. As the automated nature of the phenomenon, the need for a strong, well thought-out and reliable database arose owing to storing the data of the customers, their orders and the products any of the businesses sell. Therefore our database stores the data of all the necessary Entities involved in online shopping. As the nature of this software is online-based, the possibility of a manual data entry system becomes unviable, not to mention all the problems that follow the manual data entry system.

    o Issues with Manual System’s

    o Inefficient (Inefficient because of manual)

    o Inaccurate (Inaccurate because chance of human error)

    o Hard to Make Changes

  5. Proposed Solution

    6. o Local Delivery Management System is designed to accommodate delivery businesses in which customers have a regular “standing order” that may be modified or placed on hold from time to time. Because the Local Delivery Management System was designed for home delivery businesses that are based on the standing order, we have avoided the “shopping cart” functionality common to many ecommerce-type software packages. The shopping cart pattern has some inherent problems when used for a standing order business, and the Local Delivery Management System handles these types of recurring sales transactions much better than shopping cart-based software solutions.
